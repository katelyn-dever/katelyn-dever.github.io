who am i?

an almost 29-year-old non-traditional student in the midst of earning a bachelor's degree in information technology with a concentration in software engineering. concentration is the key word because i am much more interested in full-stack development and the world of code than i am in networks, cables, or how to operate a switch. however, i'll learn it all and become a proverbial jill-of-all-trades because, why not? i'm here to be a sponge of everything i can fit into my brain in 2 years.

all online is also a key word because we move...a LOT. i absolutely love it: traveling the country, experiencing new things, and always staying on my toes. i've also become very versatile in job skills because of this, as you may notice from my resume. from teaching to dental assisting, housekeeping to nannying, even to a brief stint in woodworking, my job has been determined by the moves we have made for my wife's career. this doesn't stop me, i passionately learn as much as i can to be the best at every job i perform. and with this decision to go back to school i'm putting an investment in my future career: one that has a market everywhere, one that is universal, one that has no limits, and one that i can participate in from any point on the globe (with Internet access).

while job experience may be something i lack, i make up for it in multitudes with unparalleled drive and determination. i go above and beyond for any task i pursue. i do my best not to simply learn new skills, but master them, engulfing information and being able to perform with expertise, creativity, and strong attention to detail.
